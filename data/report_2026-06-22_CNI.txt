# SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

**[Fact]** Railroad pricing in Canada is subject to oversight by the Canadian Transportation Agency (CTA) under the Canada Transportation Act. The Maximum Revenue Entitlement (MRE) for western grain movements caps the total revenue CN and CPKC can collect per tonne of regulated grain — set annually based on costs, inflation, and volume. For 2024–25, the MRE was revised following the Federal Court decision in 2023 that struck down certain revenue cap methodologies, creating ongoing regulatory uncertainty for ~15–20% of CN's grain revenue. **[Fact — source: Transport Canada, Federal Court ruling November 2023]**

**Pricing Power Assessment:**

| Segment | Pricing Freedom | Power Level | Evidence |
|---------|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Western grain (regulated) | Constrained by MRE | Low-medium | MRE adjusts annually; CN cannot price freely |
| Intermodal (unregulated) | Full market pricing | Medium | Competes with trucking; elasticity moderate |
| Petroleum/chemicals (unregulated) | Full market pricing | Medium-high | Long-term contracts; less elastic |
| Coal/potash (unregulated) | Full market pricing | High (captive) | No alternative rail option for many shippers |
| Automotive (unregulated) | Negotiated | Medium | OEM has optionality (CPKC) |

**[Analysis]** CN has consistently achieved revenue-per-RTM growth of 3–5% annually over 2015–2024, outpacing CPI in most years — evidence of above-inflation pricing power in unregulated segments. The grain MRE creates a ~15–20% revenue ceiling risk for the western grain segment.

**Reference Pricing / Switching Cost:** For a Prairie grain elevator shipping 100,000 MT/year, trucking to the nearest alternative port (if on-CN track) is economically non-viable; the switching cost is essentially infinite unless a CPKC connection exists at origin. For an intermodal shipper, the switching cost is low on a per-shipment basis but moderately high when factoring in IT integration and service-level agreement renegotiation.

**ARPU Ceiling:** The binding constraint on yield per RTM is the MRE (grain) and trucking economics (intermodal). The current revenue per RTM of approximately CAD 4.8 cents/RTM (calculated: CAD 16,835M ÷ ~350B RTM estimated 2024) has room to grow ~3–5% annually before triggering meaningful demand destruction in unregulated segments.
