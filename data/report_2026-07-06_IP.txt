**Analysis.** The primary procurement blocker is price vs. incumbent relationships. For new business, IP must demonstrate cost-competitiveness vs. regional independents. ESG certifications (SFI, FSC) are increasingly table-stakes requirements.

# SECTION 11: UNIT ECONOMICS AND EFFICIENCY

## Mill-Level Economics

**Fact.** IP's North American containerboard mills have average cash costs of approximately $400–500/short ton (fiber + energy + labor + chemicals). At $660/ton realized price, gross cash margin ~$160–260/ton. Converting costs add ~$100–120/ton. Box-level EBITDA margin: ~10–15%.

**Calculation:**
- Revenue per box (assuming avg. 1.5 sq ft box at $0.35/sq ft ASP): ~$0.52/box
- Material cost (linerboard + medium at $500/ton, ~0.1 lbs/sq ft): ~$0.025/sq ft = ~$0.038/box
- Total conversion cost per box: ~$0.40–0.45/box
- EBITDA per box: ~$0.07–0.10/box (~15–20% margin on material-only basis)

**Analysis.** Unit economics are highly sensitive to the linerboard price benchmark. Each $50/ton move in North American linerboard pricing equates to approximately **$400–500M in EBITDA impact** for IP at ~9–10M ton production run rate.

# SECTION 14: MOAT AND DATA ADVANTAGE

## Moat Sources

**Fact.** IP's competitive moat consists of:

1. **Cost scale in containerboard production:** IP's 9–10 integrated mills benefit from capital-intensive barriers to entry ($1.5–3B to build a new containerboard mill), decades of operational learning, and fiber sourcing advantages

2. **Vertical integration (mill-to-box):** IP can offer "system" economics — lower delivered cost of a corrugated box vs. buying containerboard on the open market. Competitors without captive mills pay $60–100/ton open-market premium

3. **Regional converting plant density:** 80+ plants in North America create geographic coverage that independents cannot replicate at scale without decades of investment

4. **Customer switching costs:** Engineered packaging solutions, food-contact certifications, and supply-chain integration create 6–18 month re-qualification hurdles

**Analysis.** The moat is **moderate and cyclically sensitive** — it provides pricing power above cash cost but does not insulate IP from commodity price cycles. The moat is **structural** (scale/integration) rather than **network effect** or **data-based**.

## Moat Deepening / Erosion

**Inference.** DS Smith strengthens the moat by creating a transatlantic integrated producer — theoretically the only company able to offer European customers NA production backup and vice versa. However, this thesis requires 3–5 years to manifest commercially.

**Primary moat collapse risk:** Smurfit WestRock using its combined scale to aggressively price-to-win in large contract renewals, eroding IP's share in premium segments.
