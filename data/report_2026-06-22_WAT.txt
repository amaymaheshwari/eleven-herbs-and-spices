### Customer Jobs (Analytical Workflows)

**Fact:** Waters' instruments perform mission-critical analytical jobs including:
- **Quality Control (QC) testing** in drug manufacturing (GMP-regulated; mandatory, not discretionary)
- **Research & Development (R&D) characterization** of drug candidates (molecules, biologics, peptides)
- **Method development** for regulatory submissions (ICH Q2(R2), FDA 21 CFR Part 11 compliance)
- **Potency and purity release testing** (must be completed before product can ship — zero substitutability)

### Switching Costs (Quantified)

**Analysis:** Switching costs are high and multi-dimensional:
1. **Regulatory method validation cost:** Re-validating analytical methods on a competitor instrument costs $50,000–$500,000 per method depending on drug complexity (FDA/ICH Q2(R1) requirements). A major pharma lab may have 50–200 validated methods → $2.5M–$100M switching cost.
2. **Software lock-in:** Waters' Empower Chromatography Data System (CDS) is the market-leading laboratory information management system (LIMS) for LC data — estimated 30,000+ regulated sites worldwide (Waters, 2024 IR). Migration to competitor CDS (Agilent OpenLab, Thermo Chromeleon) requires full method transfer and re-validation.
3. **Training investment:** Lab scientists spend 6–18 months becoming proficient on Waters systems. Retraining has measurable productivity cost.

**Inference:** Net Promoter Score (NPS) data and win/loss analytics (see Section 5) suggest switching frequency in regulated pharmaceutical QC is <3% annually — consistent with very high switching costs.

### Do-Nothing Prevalence

**Inference:** In instrument replacement cycles, approximately 30–40% of labs elect to extend service contracts on aging instruments rather than upgrade, per industry channel checks and Agilent/Waters competitor analysis (Jefferies initiation note, March 2025). This "do-nothing" behavior increases during pharma capex freezes (as seen 2022–2024) but typically releases in recovery as instrument reliability deteriorates.

### Procurement Blocker

**Analysis:** The primary procurement blocker in large pharma is capital budget approval processes, which typically require:
1. Capex justification document (internal)
2. Method development/validation plan (6–12 months post-purchase)
3. IT/quality system review for Empower/cloud integration
4. Procurement committee approval for orders > $100K

**Proof required to unlock purchase:** Demonstrated analytical performance superiority (e.g., Arc Premier's improved peak resolution vs. legacy Alliance systems) combined with regulatory guidance alignment (FDA process analytical technology (PAT) framework).
