## Development Economics

**[Fact/Analysis]** For each $1.0B of development capital deployed at 5.8% stabilized yield:
- Stabilized NOI: $58M/year
- At a 5.0% cap rate, Value = $58M / 0.05 = $1,160M → **NPV created = $160M per $1B invested**
- At a 4.75% cap rate, Value = $58M / 0.0475 = $1,221M → **NPV created = $221M per $1B invested**

This represents a **16–22% development margin** before overhead, a powerful value-creation engine.

## Operating Efficiency

**[Analysis]** AVB's Same-Store Operating Expense (OpEx) growth has historically been in the 3–5% range annually (driven by real estate taxes, insurance, and labor), which must be exceeded by revenue growth to achieve positive operating leverage. In 2024, revenue growth of approximately 2.7% vs. OpEx growth of approximately 3–4% resulted in modest operating deleverage—the single most important short-term efficiency risk.

## Structural Bottleneck

**[Analysis]** Real estate tax increases (driven by reassessments in high-value coastal markets) are the single largest constraint to improving payback/efficiency. In states like California (Proposition 13 limits escalation) and New York/Virginia (periodic reassessments), real estate tax exposure is $800–$1,200 per unit annually and grows with property values.

# SECTION 14: MOAT AND DATA ADVANTAGE

## Moat Sources

**[Analysis]** AVB's economic moat is **narrow-to-wide**, primarily derived from:

1. **Location/Land Scarcity (Wide in specific markets):** Premier locations in high-barrier coastal markets cannot be replicated; permitting and entitlement timelines of 3–7 years in Boston, D.C., and California create permanent barriers.

2. **Development Platform (Narrow-to-Wide):** In-house development expertise, architectural design, general contracting relationships, and political/regulatory capital took decades to build. This allows AVB to capture the developer profit margin that external buyers cannot.

3. **Scale Efficiency (Narrow):** ~90,000-unit scale enables centralized leasing, bulk purchasing (amenities, appliances, insurance), and technology amortization across the portfolio.

4. **Brand (Narrow):** Avalon brand recognition in coastal markets drives premium pricing of 5–10% above unbranded peers in direct comparisons.

## Evidence of Moat Deepening

- Centralized leasing reducing per-community overhead by ~20–30%
- Smart-home technology premium adoption growing
- Development pipeline delivering at stabilized yields above market cap rates

## Moat Collapse Risk

**[Inference]** The event most likely to collapse AVB's moat in 24 months: **Aggressive rent-control legislation expanding to Washington, D.C. or Northern Virginia**, combined with a structural shift to work-from-home reducing coastal demand. Either alone would impair pricing power; together they could structurally reduce NOI growth potential by 200–300 bps.
