### Switching Costs and Lock-In

*Analysis.* Switching costs in paper-based packaging are **moderate but meaningful**:

1. **Tooling and die costs:** Custom corrugated dies for specific SKUs typically cost $500–2,000 each; a large FMCG customer may have hundreds of SKUs. Switching supplier requires recreating the tooling base — typically 3–6 month lead time and meaningful cost.

2. **Supply chain integration:** SW's integrated plants (paper mill → converting plant → customer) are often co-located with major FMCG facilities. Geographic proximity creates a quasi-captive relationship.

3. **Qualification and testing:** Food-contact compliance, sustainability certifications (FSC chain of custody), and customer-specific quality standards require 2–6 month qualification periods.

4. **Contractual terms:** Major accounts typically operate on **2–5 year supply agreements** with volume commitments; early termination clauses. Spot market penetration is limited for volume customers.

*Analysis.* The "do-nothing" prevalence is **high** in this sector — packaging is typically 1–3% of COGS for most customers, making price negotiation effort difficult to justify for small savings. This provides pricing power for incumbents.

---

### Procurement Blockers

*Analysis.* The primary procurement blocker is **sustainability compliance.** Increasingly, SW must demonstrate:
- Third-party chain-of-custody certification (FSC/PEFC)
- Recyclability and compostability data (EU Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation, PPWR)
- Carbon footprint documentation (Scope 3 reporting)

SW's vertical integration from managed/certified forests (primarily in Europe and LatAm) to finished box provides a competitive advantage in meeting these requirements vs. pure converting competitors. **Proof required to unlock purchase: sustainability compliance package + total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis showing fiber-based packaging competitive with alternatives.**

## SECTION 7: GO-TO-MARKET AND DISTRIBUTION

### Demand Sources and Mix

*Fact.* SW's go-to-market (GTM) model is a **direct sales force** to large accounts supplemented by regional account managers for mid-market customers. There is no meaningful channel/distribution layer — this is a direct B2B model. Key GTM characteristics:

- **Integrated sales:** Mill-to-box integration means SW sells at the "system" level (paper + converting + design), not just commodity paper tonnage.
- **Long-term contracts:** Large customers (FMCG multinationals) typically have multi-year supply agreements; pricing is often formula-based (input cost pass-through + margin) or market-index linked.
- **Geographic proximity:** SW's manufacturing network is designed to be within 200–300 km of major customer facilities (within one-day delivery radius).

---

### Single Biggest Funnel Bottleneck

*Analysis.* The single biggest GTM bottleneck is **pricing negotiation cycle time during periods of input-cost volatility.** When OCC prices spike (as in 2021–2022) or fall sharply (as in 2023), the lag between input cost changes and contractual price resets creates margin squeeze. The 2022–2023 margin pressure was partly attributable to this lag. SW's pricing discipline and contract structure improvement is a key execution priority.

---

### What Doubling Pipeline Without Doubling Opex Requires

*Analysis.* Unlike a SaaS business, SW cannot easily "double pipeline" digitally. For SW, the equivalent question is: **How does SW grow revenue 20–30% without proportional capex?** Answer: Through (1) sweating existing assets harder (volume utilization improvement from current ~85–88% to 92–95%); (2) selective mill optimization (converting newsprint to containerboard — already underway at several WestRock facilities); (3) geographic cross-selling (SW's European relationships introducing WestRock customer solutions, and vice versa). This organic growth path is capital-efficient relative to greenfield capacity additions.

## SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

### Pricing Governance

*Analysis.* SW's pricing power is **above average for an industrial commodity** but below that of true differentiated industrial companies:

1. **Formula-based contracts:** ~40–50% of revenue has embedded input-cost pass-through mechanisms, protecting margins when inputs rise but also giving back margin uplift when inputs fall.

2. **Published price benchmarks:** North American linerboard and medium pricing is set against RISI/Fisher International published prices. European pricing follows Euro-based benchmarks. SW is a price-maker in the market given its scale — its pricing decisions influence, not just follow, industry benchmarks.

3. **Value-added differentiation:** In consumer packaging and specialty segments, SW can price on value (packaging design ROI, sustainability premium) rather than pure commodity benchmarks, supporting ~100–200 bps margin premium.

---

### Price History and Elasticity Evidence

*Fact.* Key pricing data points:
- North American linerboard (42-lb basis weight): Peaked at ~$700–750/ton in late 2022; troughed at approximately $580–590/ton in late 2023; recovered to ~$620–640/ton range in early 2025 (RISI estimates, corroborated by company commentary).
- European kraftliner: More stable; ranged from ~€650–750/ton over 2022–2024.
- Smurfit Kappa achieved a **price/mix improvement of approximately 7% in FY2023** despite volume headwinds, demonstrating relative pricing resilience in European operations.

*Source: Smurfit Kappa FY2023 Annual Report; WestRock FY2023 10-K earnings commentary; RISI public price indices.*

*Inference.* For large FMCG customers, packaging represents approximately 1–3% of COGS.
