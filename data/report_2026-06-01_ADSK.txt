## SECTION 1: THESIS FRAMING

### Value-Creation Hurdle

### Three Core Thesis Pillars (If-Then Structure)

**Pillar 1 — Subscription Transition Completion Creates Structural FCF Uplift**
- *If* Autodesk completes its multi-year shift from legacy perpetual/EBA (Enterprise Business Agreement) licenses to a pure subscription/transaction model by FY2026,

### Workflow Depth and Switching Costs

[Analysis] Autodesk's primary switching cost is **data format lock-in.** The .dwg (AutoCAD), .rvt (Revit), and .ipt/.iam (Inventor) file formats are the industry-standard exchange formats. Decades of project archives exist in these formats. A structural engineering firm with 20 years of Revit project data cannot migrate to a competitor without either (a) abandoning historical searchability or (b) investing in a multi-year conversion project. This creates a "data gravity" moat.

[Fact] AutoCAD's .dwg format is estimated to be used in approximately 90% of all 2D design files exchanged globally (Autodesk investor materials, multiple industry surveys). Even competitors (Bentley MicroStation, Bricsys BricsCAD) offer .dwg compatibility, validating its centrality.

### Pain Points and Do-Nothing Prevalence

[Analysis] The primary pain point for enterprise AEC is coordination failure — teams on different software versions, subcontractors using incompatible file formats, and owners unable to use design data for facility management. Autodesk's platform pitch is coordination-as-a-service. The "do-nothing" option is maintaining fragmented workflows; in large projects (>$100M), fragmentation costs are estimated at 2–5% of project value (McKinsey Global Institute, 2017 construction productivity report), making the ROI case for unified platforms compelling.

### Procurement Blockers

[Analysis] The key procurement blocker in enterprise is **IT security and data residency approval** (particularly in government/defense AEC), which extends sales cycles to 9–18 months. The proof required to unlock purchase is a successful pilot on a representative project (typically a 90-day proof-of-concept on Autodesk Construction Cloud for new ACC logos).

## SECTION 11: UNIT ECONOMICS AND EFFICIENCY

### CAC, LTV, and Payback

[Analysis — based on publicly available financials; precise segment-level CAC not disclosed]

**Assumptions:**
- Sales & Marketing expense FY2025: ~$1,730M
- New ARR added in FY2025: ~$650–700M (inferred from subscription growth)
- Blended gross margin: ~88%

**Calculations:**
- **CAC (blended):** S&M spend ÷ new ARR = $1,730M ÷ $675M ≈ **$2.56 per $1 of new ARR** (i.e., ~2.6× ARR)
- **Payback period (gross margin basis):** CAC ÷ (New ARR × GM%) = $2.56 ÷ ($1.00 × 0.88) = **~2.9 years**
- **LTV (at 5% annual churn, 10-year horizon, 88% GM):** $1.00 ARR × 88% × (1/5%) = **$17.60 LTV per $1 ARR**
- **LTV/CAC ratio:** $17.60 ÷ $2.56 = **~6.9×** — healthy (>3× is generally considered good)

[Analysis] The ~2.9-year payback period is somewhat elevated vs. best-in-class SaaS (1.5–2 years), reflecting the high cost of enterprise field sales and the complexity of the transition period. As direct billing matures and self-serve grows, payback improvement toward 2.0–2.5 years is achievable by FY2027.

### Structurally Unprofitable Cohorts

[Analysis] SMB/individual users acquired via heavy discounting or promotional pricing during FY2020–FY2022 (COVID-era promotions, educational program upgrades) likely have CAC payback periods >4 years and may be structurally marginal. Management has not disclosed this cohort's economics, which is a **data gap**.
