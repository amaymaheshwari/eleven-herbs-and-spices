# SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

**Fact:** In the AI server market, pricing is primarily driven by component costs (GPU costs, DRAM, power components) with server manufacturers adding a margin layer. SMCI's pricing is **cost-plus with competitive benchmarking**.

**Analysis:**
- SMCI has demonstrated **limited independent pricing power** — its 13.5% gross margin in FY2024 vs. 18.0% in FY2023 reflects pricing concessions made to win market share in the AI server land-grab
- The hyperscaler procurement process is highly competitive: large deals are typically bid processes where SMCI, Dell, ODMs, and others compete on TCO (total cost of ownership)
- SMCI's DLC premium (estimated 200–400 bps gross margin advantage vs. air-cooled systems) is currently being competed away as power constraints force all vendors to adopt liquid cooling

**Pricing Elasticity Evidence:**
- When NVIDIA GPU prices increased 20–30% in H2 2023 due to demand surge, SMCI passed through cost increases largely 1:1, maintaining dollar margins but compressing percentage margins
- SMCI's ARPU ceiling before churn inflects: **estimated ~$50,000 per server unit** for standard configurations; custom/DLC systems command $70,000–120,000+

**Monetization Change Log:**
- FY2023: Gross margin ~18% — period of strong pricing discipline
- FY2024: Gross margin ~13.5% — aggressive pricing to capture AI server market share
- FY2025 Q2: Gross margin ~11.9% — further compression (reported February 2025 earnings)
