## SECTION 7: GO-TO-MARKET AND DISTRIBUTION

**[Fact]** Silgan does not have a traditional "sales force" in the software sense. Customer acquisition is relationship-driven, contract-based, and infrequent (once per multi-year cycle). The GTM model is:

**Doubling pipeline without doubling opex:** Silgan's capacity is the constraint, not sales effort. Growth requires capital investment (new lines) or acquisition, not incremental sales headcount.

## SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

**[Fact]** Silgan's pricing model is largely **cost-plus pass-through** in Metal Containers:
- Steel tinplate costs are passed through to customers, typically with a 30–90 day lag
- The "spread" (conversion margin) is relatively stable; Silgan earns on volume and efficiency, not steel speculation
- In Closures and Dispensing, there is more pricing power (value-based components)

**[Analysis]** This pass-through model has two important implications:
1. **Upside limitation:** Silgan doesn't benefit from steel deflation (revenues fall with costs, preserving margin in % but not $)
2. **Downside protection:** Steel inflation hurts revenue growth optics but doesn't impair margins meaningfully (assuming contracts function as designed)
