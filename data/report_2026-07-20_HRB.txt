## SECTION 1: THESIS FRAMING

In FY2024, HRB reported total revenue of $3.58 billion and net income of $622 million (10-K, FY2024, filed June 2024).

**[Fact]** The stock trades at approximately $67 (June 2025), implying ~12.5× trailing earnings and ~8.5× trailing EBITDA—a meaningful discount to both its own 5-year historical average (~14× P/E) and to specialty financial services peers.

- ~6% (~10M) were prepared by free/volunteer services (VITA, AARP, IRS Direct File pilot)

**[Fact]** The US tax preparation services market was valued at approximately $14–15 billion in 2024 (IBISWorld, Tax Preparation Services in the US, 2024 report). This encompasses assisted professional preparation and DIY software.

### Growth Drivers and Constraints

**[Analysis]** The overall US tax filing population grows slowly (~1–2% per year) with demographic shifts. The primary growth lever for HRB is ARPC expansion (pricing) rather than volume growth. The binding constraint is:
- **Demand side**: Relatively stable total return population; shift from assisted to DIY is a structural secular headwind of ~1–2% per year
- **Supply side**: HRB has adequate capacity; the constraint is retaining preparers (labor) and retaining clients from DIY migration

**TAM Shrinkage Scenario:** If IRS Direct File reaches 15–20M returns by 2028 (bear case), effective assisted TAM shrinks by ~5–8%, and simple DIY TAM shrinks by ~10–15%. HRB's exposure is concentrated in assisted, where Direct File threat is structurally lower.

## SECTION 3: CUSTOMER SEGMENTS AND JOBS-TO-BE-DONE

### Customer Mix

**[Fact]** HRB's assisted-filing customer demographic skews toward:
- Households with income $25,000–$75,000 (EITC-eligible range)
- Filers with moderate complexity: multiple W-2s, self-employment income, rental income, life events (marriage, divorce, home purchase)
- Geographic concentration in middle America, suburban/rural markets
- Age: 35–65 (loyalty-driven, lower digital-native propensity)

**[Analysis]** The "job-to-be-done" for HRB's core assisted customer is: *"Maximize my refund and ensure compliance while minimizing my cognitive burden."* This differs from a simple tax compliance job; it includes:
1. Refund anticipation / cash-flow management (Emerald Card, Refund Advance)
2. Audit protection and Peace of Mind guarantee
3. Year-round accessibility for tax questions (Block Advantage membership)

### Switching Costs

| Switching Cost Type | Strength | Evidence |
|--------------------|---------|---------|
| Habitual loyalty | High | ~70% of clients return annually (HRB IR, FY2024 earnings call, June 2024) |
| Data continuity (prior-year return on file) | Moderate | Reduces friction; competitors can import |
| Trust/relationship with individual preparer | High | Particularly in franchise locations |
| Financial products integration (Emerald Card) | Moderate | Banked population already integrated |

**[Inference]** Switching costs are behavioral/habitual rather than technical—making them real but potentially fragile if a compelling alternative (better price, IRS Direct File) emerges.

### Do-Nothing Prevalence

**[Analysis]** Approximately 30–35% of eligible EITC filers leave money on the table annually due to complexity aversion (IRS EITC participation rate data, 2023). HRB's "do-nothing" competitor is also the unbanked/underserved population that avoids formal tax prep entirely. This represents an addressable but historically difficult acquisition target.

### Procurement Blocker

For retail assisted: No formal procurement; decision is individual/household. Primary proof required: trust signal (brand recognition, physical presence, guarantee language) and price clarity. The Refund Advance product (up to $3,500 interest-free) serves as a powerful trial mechanic that converts price-sensitive customers.
