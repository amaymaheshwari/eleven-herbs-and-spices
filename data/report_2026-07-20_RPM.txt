## Pain Points and Workflow Mapping

**[Analysis]**
- **Industrial/OEM:** Pain point = asset protection (corrosion, chemical exposure, UV degradation). Job-to-be-done: coat, protect, and maintain assets with minimal downtime and regulatory compliance. RPM's Carboline (PCG) and Rust-Oleum industrial lines are deeply embedded in maintenance schedules.
- **Contractors/Applicators:** Pain point = reliable product performance, technical support, warranty. Job: apply coatings that pass inspection and hold up under warranty. Tremco's roofing and waterproofing warranties create multi-year lock-in.
- **DIY/Consumer:** Pain point = ease of use, finish quality, availability. Rust-Oleum and DAP are trusted brands at retail. Switching cost is low — brand loyalty is real but fragile; private-label competition is persistent.
- **Government/Infrastructure:** Pain point = compliance, longevity, performance certification. Procurement cycles are long (6–24 months) but contracts are sticky once won.

## Switching Costs (Quantified Where Possible)
**[Analysis]** In industrial maintenance, changing coating suppliers typically requires re-qualification testing ($50K–$500K per facility), regulatory resubmission in some end-uses, and retraining of maintenance crews. For Tremco roofing systems with 10–20 year warranties, the cost to switch mid-warranty is effectively 100% of the warranty value. Estimated switching cost for institutional/industrial customers: **equivalent to 2–4 years of product spend**.

## Do-Nothing Prevalence & Procurement Blocker
**[Inference]** In industrial MRO, deferred maintenance is common during downturns — customers extend coating cycles from 5 to 7+ years, reducing volume. The procurement blocker in government/infrastructure is the lengthy specification and approval process; RPM's brand (Tremco, Nudura, Stonhard) being pre-approved is a significant competitive moat.

## Succession Risk — Single Leadership Gap
**[Analysis]** **Frank Sullivan is the existential succession risk.** He is the architect of RPM's strategy, culture, and MAP framework. No identified public successor has been announced. Age is not disclosed in public filings, but he has been CEO for 23 years. Unplanned departure would likely cause a 10–15% stock correction. **This is the top governance risk.**

## Organizational Design
**[Analysis]** RPM operates a **decentralized multi-brand model** — operating units retain local management autonomy while corporate provides financial discipline, shared services, and procurement leverage. This model maximizes brand-level responsiveness but limits synergy capture. MAP 2025 has pushed more centralization in procurement, HR, and IT — a healthy evolution.

## Operating Cadence Metric That Best Predicts Misses
**[Inference]** **Working capital as a % of revenue** (quarterly) has been the best leading indicator of earnings misses. When receivables and inventory build faster than revenue, it typically presages margin pressure and FCF disappointment 1–2 quarters later. Target: working capital/revenue < 18%; Red flag: > 22%.
