### Switching Costs

[A] Switching costs are high for long-tenured advisory relationships because:
- Multi-year project continuity: an average major infrastructure project runs 3–10 years; mid-project firm substitution is extremely costly (mobilization, knowledge transfer, permitting restarts)
- Proprietary data: Jacobs retains institutional knowledge of site conditions, regulatory history, and agency preferences
- Cleared personnel: Government security-clearance holders cannot be substituted without 6–18 month re-clearance timelines
- PA Consulting's embedded advisory relationships in Whitehall/UK public sector are particularly sticky

[I] Estimated switching cost equivalent: 8–15% of total project cost, implying a structurally high renewal rate of >85% on active projects.

### Do-Nothing Prevalence and Procurement Blockers

[A] Do-nothing is relatively rare in Jacobs' core markets because:
- Regulatory mandates (EPA consent decrees, FHWA requirements) force agency action
- IIJA and CHIPS Act create use-it-or-lose-it funding windows

**Key procurement blocker:** Federal and state procurement cycles average 12–24 months from Request for Proposal (RFP) to Notice to Proceed (NTP). Budget appropriation delays (Continuing Resolutions (CRs)) create lumpiness. The single most common unlock is a signed appropriations bill or agency task order issuance.
