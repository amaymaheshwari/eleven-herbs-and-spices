# SECTION 17: SUPPLY CHAIN AND OPERATIONS

**Analysis.** As a banking institution, FFIN's "supply chain" consists of: (1) funding sources (deposits, FHLB, capital markets); (2) technology vendors (FiServ, Jack Henry); (3) human capital (relationship managers, credit officers, compliance staff); (4) physical branch network.

**Fact.** FFIN's technology vendor concentration (FiServ/Jack Henry) represents a single-point-of-failure risk for core banking operations. Any disruption to these platforms could impair transaction processing, deposit servicing, and loan management.

**Fact.** The Texas job market for experienced commercial bankers is highly competitive, with unemployment in financial services below 3% (BLS Texas data, 2024). This constrains FFIN's ability to rapidly staff growth markets.

**Analysis.** Manufacturing economics analog: branch operating costs are largely fixed (~$800K–$1.2M per branch annually, including staff, lease, technology), creating high operating leverage as deposit/loan volumes grow. Branches in West Texas legacy markets likely operate at 3–5× their annual cost in revenue — highly efficient. New DFW/Houston branches require 2–4 years to reach breakeven.

**Inference.** Single point of failure: FiServ core banking platform outage. Mitigation: FFIN maintains standard business continuity protocols but has not disclosed specific Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) publicly.

**Path:** (1) Fed delivers 2 rate cuts by Q1 2026; (2) Texas loan growth resumes at 5–6% YoY; (3) NIM stabilizes at 3.40–3.55%; (4) NCO rate stays <0.20%; (5) EPS grows to $1.57 (2025) and $1.68 (2026).

**Path:** (1) Fed delivers 3–4 cuts; (2) Texas GDP accelerates to 5%+ growth; (3) FFIN completes accretive acquisition; (4) NIM expands to 3.70%; (5) EPS reaches $1.95–2.00 by 2026; (6) Market awards 22× P/E.

**Outcome:** Price target ~$42.90–44.00. Total return: **+39.4%** + ~3% dividend = **~+42.4%**.
