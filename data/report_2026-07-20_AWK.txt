### Key Accounting Judgment: AFUDC

**Fact.** AFUDC (Allowance for Funds Used During Construction) is a non-cash credit to income representing the cost of financing construction in progress. In 2024E, AFUDC equity component was approximately ~$100–120M (estimate based on prior years and growing WIP). This **inflates GAAP net income** relative to cash earnings — an important bridge item.

- AFUDC as % of net income: ~10–12% → removes ~$0.55–0.65/share from "cash" EPS
- Cash EPS (ex-AFUDC): ~$5.00–5.10 vs. reported ~$5.55–5.65 (2024E)

**Analysis.** This is a legitimate, well-understood utility accounting convention, but it means AWK's reported EPS overstates cash generation capacity. The valuation framework should apply P/E to reported EPS but acknowledge cash yield is lower.
