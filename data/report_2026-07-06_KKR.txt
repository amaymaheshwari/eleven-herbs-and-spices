**[Analysis]** Minimum-viable ecosystem health metric for KKR: **LP re-up rate > 70% across flagship funds**. Below this level, AUM compounding decelerates and management fee growth slows materially.

**[Inference]** Doubling pipeline without doubling opex requires: (1) digitizing LP onboarding for retail/HNW; (2) leveraging Capital Group's distribution infrastructure (no incremental headcount for KKR); (3) launching additional semi-liquid/evergreen structures that don't require 10-year blind-pool commitments.

## SECTION 10: PRICING POWER AND ELASTICITY TESTING

**[Analysis]** KKR's pricing power is a function of fund performance relative to public market equivalents.

**[Fact]** KKR Americas XII (PE) charges a 1.5% management fee on committed capital and a 20% carried interest above an 8% hurdle rate. This is the industry standard "2-and-20" structure, albeit now closer to "1.5-and-20" for large institutional commitments (KKR Fund Documents, SEC Form D filings, 2023–2024).

**[Fact]** KKR's flagship PE funds have generated net IRRs of approximately 16–18% historically (KKR 2024 Annual Report, Performance Data). At these return levels, the 20% carry represents ~200–360 basis points (bps) of annual return capture — delivering strong value to LPs who earn 13–15% net, well above public market equivalents.

### Pricing Sensitivity Analysis

| Scenario | Blended Mgmt Fee Rate | Annual Mgmt Fees at $700B FEAUM | Delta vs. Base |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bear (fee compression −20 bps) | 0.47% | $3.29B | −$420M |
| Base | 0.53% | $3.71B | — |
| Bull (rate flat + AUM growth) | 0.53% | $4.24B (at $800B FEAUM) | +$530M |

**[Analysis]** ARPU ceiling before churn inflects: empirically, LP sensitivity to management fee increases is low for >15% net IRR performance. Below 12% net IRR, LP pressure on fees increases significantly. The reference price/switching cost: a 25 bps fee reduction saves an LP ~$2.5M per $1B committed — material but insufficient to offset a multi-year relationship switch cost of $10–20M in operations and performance drag.

**[Inference]** KKR has limited ability to raise management fees unilaterally (pricing power is asymmetric — it flows through performance, not list prices). The real pricing power is in **launching new product types** at market rates into underpenetrated segments (retail, infrastructure) rather than repricing existing funds.
