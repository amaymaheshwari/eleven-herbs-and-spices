[Analysis]

### Competitive Dynamics

**Ratings duopoly:** [Fact] SPGI and Moody's collectively hold ~80%+ of global credit rating market share by revenue (SEC annual study on NRSRO competition, 2023). Regulatory barriers (NRSRO designation) effectively limit new entrants. Fitch competes on structured finance and European sovereigns but has not materially gained share on SPGI in investment-grade corporates.

**Market Intelligence:** [Fact] Bloomberg Terminal retains dominance in real-time trading data (~$7B revenue, ~330,000 terminals). SPGI's Capital IQ Pro competes on corporate fundamentals, credit analytics, and deal data—not real-time tick data. The segments are partially complementary, reducing direct substitution risk. However, LSEG's Workspace (formerly Refinitiv Eikon) is aggressively competing for analytics wallet share post-2022 LSEG transformation.

**Indices:** [Analysis] MSCI is the primary competitor in factor/ESG indices and international equity benchmarks. SPGI dominates U.S. equity benchmarks. The two rarely compete head-to-head for the same ETF mandate. MSCI's ESG ratings business (acquired from MSCI in a different context) does create overlap in ESG data products with SPGI's ESG & Climate segment.

### Win/Loss Reasons
[Inference based on sell-side channel checks and industry reports]
- **Win:** Regulatory embedding (Platts, ratings), workflow depth (Capital IQ SNL in banking), brand trust
- **Lose:** Real-time data (Bloomberg), international fixed income analytics (LSEG), ESG scoring breadth (MSCI, Sustainalytics/Morningstar)
- **Competitor responses:** Bloomberg is investing in alternative data and credit analytics (poaching SPGI MI talent); MSCI is expanding private assets coverage; LSEG is bundling Workspace + clearing data as value proposition
