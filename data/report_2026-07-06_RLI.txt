**[Inference]** In specialty/E&S lines, demand is relatively inelastic because: (1) coverage is often legally or contractually mandated; (2) there are few alternatives; and (3) the cost of insurance is often a small % of total project/contract value. ### ARPU Ceiling

**[Analysis]** For specialty casualty lines, the ARPU ceiling is implicitly defined by the point at which large commercial insureds find captive insurance or alternative risk transfer (ART) more economical — typically when specialty premiums exceed ~2–3% of annual revenues for the insured. We estimate RLI is well below this threshold for most of its book.

- Underwriting profit = NPE × (1 - CR) = $1,410M × 12% = ~$169M
- Total pre-tax economic profit from core operations = ~$340M

**[Analysis]** RLI historically exits lines when they become unprofitable — evidence from 10-K disclosures: exited certain commercial auto lines (2018–2019), reduced Gulf Coast property exposure (2005–2006 post-Katrina). This active portfolio management prevents sustained structural unprofitability. Current watch areas: cyber liability (loss ratio pressure industry-wide) and excess casualty (social inflation impact).

## SECTION 17: SUPPLY CHAIN AND OPERATIONS

**[Analysis]** RLI's "supply chain" is reinsurance — the mechanism by which it cedes (transfers) a portion of insurance risk to global reinsurers to limit catastrophic loss exposure. This is the primary operational risk management tool.

### Reinsurance Program

**[Fact]** Per 2024 10-K, RLI purchases catastrophe reinsurance protecting its Property segment from large single-event losses. Key details (estimated from 10-K disclosures):
- Catastrophe excess-of-loss reinsurance: retention of approximately $20–30M per event; recoveries up to several hundred million dollars
- Reinsurers: primarily AM Best A-rated or better (Munich Re, Swiss Re, Hannover Re, Lloyd's of London syndicates)
- No single reinsurer represents >10% of total ceded premium (per 10-K)

**[Analysis]** Reinsurance counterparty diversification is strong. Principal risk: a simultaneous failure of multiple large reinsurers in a catastrophic event (tail risk; estimated probability <0.1% annually). More realistic risk: reinsurance cost increases sharply in a hard cat market (2022–2023 saw 30–50% cat reinsurance rate increases), squeezing RLI's net underwriting margin on Property.

### Operational Risk: Claims Management

**[Inference]** Claims management quality is a competitive differentiator — slow or unfair claims handling creates agent/broker defection and regulatory risk. RLI's low loss ratio is partly a function of claims management discipline (early settlement of meritorious claims, vigorous defense of questionable claims).

### Single Point of Failure

**[Inference]** Potential single point of failure: **RLI's concentration of underwriting talent in Peoria, IL**. A talent exodus (competitive raiding by Lloyd's or Bermuda-based insurers offering higher compensation) could erode the underwriting edge. Mitigant: RLI's compensation structure includes long-vesting equity and profit-sharing tied to underwriting results — aligning underwriter incentives with long-term performance.
